Lu’an Guapian Tea

Lu’an Guapian Tea is a famous historical tea in China and one of the top ten historical teas in China. It is called Guapian for short. It was called “Luzhou Lu 'an Tea” in Tang Dynasty and it was called “Lu’an Guapian Tea” in Ming Dynasty, which was the top grade, extremely the taste of tea. It was imperial tribute tea in Qing Dynasty. Lu 'an Guapian Tea (also known as Pian Tea) is special kind in green tea. Picking from the local unique varieties, the tea leaves are shaped like melon seeds through the unique traditional processing technology by pulling the leaves and picking off the tender buds and tea stalks. Because its shape is complete, smooth and straight and resembles sunflower seeds, and it is produced in the area of Lu’an, it is called “Lu’an Guapian Tea”.

Its main producing area is Yu’an District, Lu’an City, Anhui Province. Among them, Qishan, Huangshi, Lichong, Huangchaojian, Yu’an District and Red Stone areas of Lu’an Guapian Tea are of good quality.

The shape of Lu’an Guapian Tea is strip, the size is uniform and the cable is tight. Premium Lu’an Guapian Tea are deep green and consistent in color and lustre. It should have the fragrance of tea through by smelling. If the smell of is like burning chestnut, it would be superior. If it has the smell of grass, it indicates a lack of cooking time.

After the dry tea is made into soup with boiling water, it floats on the top at first. As the leaves open the soup, they sink to the bottom of the cup one by one. The original strip is developed into a blade and the size of blade is nearly the same, and the pieces are superimposed.

Close to the top of the cup, the mellow flavor of the tea can be tasted by its aroma concentration. Among them, the fresh fragrance belongs to the early tea with high tenderness, the chestnut fragrance belongs to the middle tea, and the high fire tea belongs to the late tea.

Use the bowl cover to move the tea and check the color of the soup. It is generally green, clear and refreshing, without a little turbidity.

The color of its leaves is generally made of tea grass ten days before the Grain Rain. After processing, the color of the leaves is light green, cyan and asymmetrical. The made after Grain Rain, the color of leaves is generally green or dark green, and symmetrical and the tea soup is also thicker. If the time is a little longer, the green would be a little deeper. After drinking two sips of tea slowly, and then sipping delicately, it is slightly bitter, cool and sweet. Tea leaves with rich nutritional and brewing tea soup often can make you feel the flexibility of tea soup.

It is said that Lu’an Guapian Tea is derived from magic tea. According to the tale, there was a farmer named Hu Lin in Mabu Town of Jinzhai, who was good at tea making. One day, he went to Qiyun Mountain to pick tea for his employer. He came to a cliff with full of tall, old trees and few people. All of a sudden, he found several exotic tea trees among the stone walls, luxurant and verdant. The buds and leaves were covered with a layer of white fume, shining with silver light, which was clearly different from other tea trees. Hu Lin was very good at distinguishing the quality of tea trees. After observing them carefully, he knew that the tea trees in front of him were extremely rare and precious varieties. So, he carefully picked fresh leaves, fried them into tea and brought it down the mountain to go home. He stopped at a teahouse because of he dad been tired for all the work he had done. Out of curiosity, he took out the tea he had with him to brew. As soon as the boiling water being poured into the teacup, a layer of foam could be seen floating in the teacup, just like the auspicious clouds fluttering, and like the golden lotus in full bloom, and suddenly the aroma was permeating all over the house. The guests in the teahouse were attracted by the aroma and agreed with one voice: “Good tea!” Seeing how delicious the tea was, Hu Lin went back to the mountain to look for the tea trees he had found among the cliffs. But once again, it was nowhere to be found. Locals consider it a “magic tea” that cannot be recovered. Some years after this story was spread, some tea trees were found in the bat cave in Qi Mountain. It was said that the tea trees were born by bats. These tea trees are exactly the same as those described by Hu Lin, so people naturally call them “magic tea”.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
