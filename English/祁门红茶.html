Keemun

keemun, with its alias as keemun Gongfu tea, is a traditional Chinese black tea. It is known for its aromatic fragrance, mellow flavor and beautiful aesthetic value as well as the brilliant red liquor the brewed tea leaves produce. Though the black tea only has a relatively short history of about 150 years, it is highly regarded as a top quality black tea. As a traditional Chinese export product, keemun, in globe, enjoys a great reputation. It is is one of the world’s three high-aroma teas; another two is Darjeeling tea from India and Ceylon Uva black tea from Sir Lanka.

keemun is mainly produced in Qinmen county, Anhui province, China, and its adjacent counties like Shitai, Dongzhi, Yi and Guichi also have a small amount of production. Qimen county, with its magnificent mountains, dense forests, fertile soil, warm climate produce the finest quality of Keemun tea. The tea gardens are usually located in places where exist natural barriers like mountains to protect tea three against extreme climate. In the gardens the soil acidity is moderate and water is rich.

Keemun has a very thin, wiry and tightly twisted leaf that is black with gray hues. Its shining black lustre is hailed as “ the light from jewelry”. It brings a unique, slightly flavor and aroma which is called “Keemun aroma”. It smells like fruit or orchid fragrance./p>

Keemun black tea is famous for its excellent infusion color( from intense orange to amber and red) and complex flavor. The tea could be steeped for three times. And each time bestows a unique flavor to the decoction. The best way to appreciate the tea is to slow down and kick up your feet and sip it gently.

Before the years of the Reign of Emperor Guangxu in the Qing Dynasty (about before 1871), the entire Keemun tea producing region only produce green teas, called “ Anlü” green tea, which boast great quality. It is widely believed that the credit for the creation of Kemmun should go to Yu Ganchen, an Anhui merchant. He perhaps traveled to the Fujian Province in a bid to know more about secrets about high quality black tea production. After he gathered enough knowledge, he produce famous Keemun black tea by simulating special techniques and methods that was adopted and mastered from the farmers of Fujian province. The tea was exquisite and unique and therefore it did not take too much of time to become hugely popular.

Anther said, according to the second issue of Agricultural and Commercial Bulletin in 1916, it was Hu Yuanlong (also known as Hu Yangru) who reformed the techniques and produce Keemun. Hu, a native of Qinmen, brought more than 5,000 acres of barren mountain in Keemun under cultivation, mainly fostering tea tree. As green tea sales slumped in , Hu studied the method of making black tea and raised money to construct a tea factory in order to convert to making black tea. At the same time, he taught the methods to farmers, so for more than 40 years, tirelessly.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
