Jingting Lü Xue Tea

Jingting Lü Xue was created in Ming Dynasty. Its place of origin is Jingting Mountain, hailed as “poem mountain” because many famous poets has written poems about this mountain. Across the area, there are many magnificent cliffs shrouded by clouds and fogs all the year. The moist climate and rich soil ensure a great ecological environment for Jingting Lü Xue, which bestow a unique flavor on the tea. There is a poem praising its unique shape and flavor.

Jingting Lü Xue comes from Jingting Mountain, north to the Xuancheng City, Anhui Province. In this area there are many cliffs and precipices covered by fogs and clouds. The humid climate and fertile soil provide a good ecological environment for high-quality teas. The tea gardens are usually located on the docks in the mountain where forests are dense and thick, thus the tea trees are free from strong and intense sunlight. The loose and rich soil ensure a prosperity of tea growth.

From the appearance, Jingting Lü Xue is shaped as sparrow’s tongue. The emerald-green leaves are strong and straight covered with white downs. Jingting Lü Xue can be subdivided into more grades according to their qualities. The aroma also changes with nuance of growth environments, which includes the fragrance of Chinese chestnuts, the orchid and honeysuckle.

After brewed, the leaves, like blossomed flowers, sink vertically to the bottom while the white down will slowly fall as snowflakes dance in the green shade. The tea soup is clear and bright with long-lasting aroma. The taste is mellow and fresh and the remained leaves are tender and keep flower-shaped.

Long long ago, there was a clever, beautiful and kind girl whose name was Lü Xue. She, long with her ill mother, lived at the foot of the Jingting Mountain. In order to earn money to have her mother cured, every year she climbed the mountain to pick the tea which grows near cliffs. One day, she found a luxuriant tea tree with verdant green leaves on the edge of a cliff. With excitement, she climbed the cliff and reach out to pick. Unfortunately, One slip, she fell off the cliff and the tea leaves she had picked in her back basket spread like green snow in the air. Magically, the leaves dropped on the land instantly grew up into great tea trees. The trees form a large tea garden so that people would never suffer the danger and pain in picking tea from cliff. To commemorate this diligent and kind girl, people give the name “Jingting Lü Xue” to the tea.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
