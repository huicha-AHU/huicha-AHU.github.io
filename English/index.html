  • Tea-Producing Regions In Anhui

    In Hilly Area in South Yangtze Basin

    The tea-producing region in hilly area in south Yangtze basin is located in the south of the Yangtze River, north of the Yellow Mountain and the western extension of the Tianmu Mountain, including Xuancheng city, Wuhu city, Tongling city and other cities. It belongs to the south hilly and low mountainous area of China’s subtropical region. This region has a mild climate, abundant rainfall and wide distribution of red and yellow soil, which is suitable for the growth of tea trees. Representative kinds of tea produced from here include Jingting Green Snow Tea, Yongxi Huoqing Tea, Jiuhua Maofeng Tea and so on.

    In Yellow Mountain Area in Southern Anhui

    The tea-producing region in Yellow Mountain area in southern Anhui is located between the Tianmu Mountain Zone and Jiuhua Mountain Zone, which is in the mountainous areas and hills centered on the Yellow Mountain Zone, including Huangshan City and Chizhou City. The climate here belongs to the subtropical humid monsoon climate with suitable growing season temperature for tea trees. The rain here is foggy and thick and the soil here is mainly acidic red soil, especially suitable for the growth of tea trees. Therefore, most famous kingds of Hui tea are produced in this areas. The tea-producing region in Yellow Mountain area is the main production area of green tea, black tea and dark tea in Anhui. Representative kinds of tea produced from here include Yellow Mountain Maofeng Tea, Tunxi Green Tea, Taiping Houkui Tea, Qimen Black Tea, Xiuning Pine Tea, Bamboo Leaf Dafang Tea, etc.

    In Dabie Mountain area in West Anhui

    The tea-producing region in Dabie Mountain area is located in the west of Anhui Province and is between Jianghuai city and Lu’an city and Anqing city, which is one of the ancient tea-producing regions in China. There are a moderate altitude and a mild climate in this region, where most soil here is yellow brown soil in the mountains and the deep soil layer is permeable to both air and water, particularly suitable for the growth of tea trees. Representative kinds of tea produced from here include Lu’an Guapian Tea, Yellow Mountain Yellow Bud Tea, Yuexi Cui Lan Tea and Tianzhu Jian Hao Tea.

    In Low Hilly Area in Yangtze-Huaihe River Valleys Area

    The tea-producing region in low hilly are in Yangtze-Huaihe River Valleys area is located in the south of the Huaihe River, north of the Yangtze River, east of the Dabie Mountains in hilly areas, involving Chuxian county, Laian county, Dingyuan county, Quanjiao county, Fengyang county and other counties. The soil in this area is mainly yellow brown soil, and the yield of tea is relatively small, among which the fried green is the most, and the representative famous kinds of tea include North Qiao Tea and South Qiao Tea of Quanjiao.

A Brief History of Hui Tea

In Three Kingdoms of Eastern Han Dynasty, Western and Eastern Jin Dynasties and Six Dynasties

At the end of the Eastern Han Dynasty, tea was spread from Sichuan Province to northern Anhui Province through Henan Province, Shaanxi Province, which was initially used for medicine only. During the Three Kingdoms Dynasty, tea spread to Xuancheng city in southern Anhui Province. In Western and Eastern Jin Dynasties and Six Dynasties, scholar-officials of the landlord class and monks advocated drinking tea, which became a popular practice.

Sui and Tang Dynasties

After the Sui and Tang Dynasties, the economic center of gravity was transferred from the Yellow River Basin to the Jianghuai River Basin, and the production of tea in Anhui Province got development opportunities. The production increased dramatically. In addition to self-use, tea also became a new commodity, which was exported to North China and the northwest frontier in large quantities.
