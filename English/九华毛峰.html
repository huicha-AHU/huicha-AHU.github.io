Jiuhua Mao Feng Tea

Jiuhua Mao Feng is a traditional Chinese green tea, created in the early Qing Dynasty. Jiuhua Mao Feng, also known as Huangshixi Mao Feng, only inferior to Huangshan Mao Feng ( a premium green tea in China) in quality, was awarded the Golden Quality Award of Panama World Expo in 1915.

The places of production are spread about Jiuhua Mountain, including Huangshixi, Minyuan, Kecun, Ducun and Linyan, etc.. Jiuhua Mountain boasts many marvelous peaks, bubbling springs, magnificent waterfalls, luxuriant vegetation, and verdant bamboo woods. It is cloudy and foggy and people can enjoy the spectacular sea of clouds on the top of mountain. Jiuhua Mountain owns the feature of subtropic monsoon climate and alpine climate. It is moist and rains a lot on the mountain with an average rainfall of 1500mm ad an average temperature of 16℃. The soil is fertile and good at water drainage. The tea gardens are mainly spotted at valleys which are 500-700 meters above the sea level.

The green-yellowish leaves are long and fine, yielding a luminous, slightly sweet liquor with intense aroma.

The tea is graded into 3 levels. The long, twisted leaves release gently during steeping. The pale gold liquor is light and smooth with sweet vegetal notes and hints of flower in every sip. The finish is clean and crisp. It still boasts its fragrance even after 5-6 times steeping.

Jiuhua Mountain is one of the four scared mountains of Chinese Buddhism. It was said that in the late Tang Dynasty (AD901-904), a Sichuan monk and a Daoist were on a pilgrimage to Jiuhua Mountain to worship Ksitigarbha Bodhisattva. During their visit they only ate wild grass and fruit as a way to show faithfulness. Days after days, they became very weak and dizzy and their bodies began to show the symptom of dropsy. So they searched on the mountain for some herbs to cure themselves. At last, they found a tea which was brought back. After they drunk the brew, their symptoms were alleviated and they gradually recovered. This kind of tea is Jiuhua Mao Feng.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
