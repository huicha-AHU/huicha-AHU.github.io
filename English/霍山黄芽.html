HuoShan Huangya Tea

Huoshan Yellow bud tea, as the Huoshan specialty of Anhui Province, is assessed as a representative product of National Geography. According to its quality, it is divided into superfine, special, first and second level. The shape is straight and slightly expanded; the color is yellow-green; the aroma is long-lasting; the tea soup is yellow and green bright; the taste is mellow and fresh; the finish is yellowish and bright. In December 2006, Huoshan Yellow bud tea was successfully awarded the title of the protection product of National Geography.号。

The authentic HuoShan Huangya is produced in Huoshan county, Lu’an city, Anhui province. You can find the best especially in the Houshan Mountain area where is well-known for its marvelous peaks, impenetrable forests and babbling brooks. Since the average temperature is 15℃ and annual precipitation is 1400mm, the climate is warm and humid, suitable for the growth of tea trees.

The shape of the tea is straight and slightly spread, shaped like a sparrow tongue. The tender buds are green but yellowish, covered with white downs called bai hao (“white hair”) because they are still so young.

The leaves reveal a very mellow and fresh taste accompanied by an unusual green-yellow color of the brew when steeped. The fragrance is long-lasting. And after brewing the leaves turn bright yellow and tender.

It is said that Princess Li Cuilian, a sister of emperor Tang Taizong in Tang dynasty, converted to Buddhism and became a abbess in a nunnery located in Huoshan Mountain. The princess favored drinking tea from an early age and she almost had drunk all the famous teas across the country. Since Huoshan is famous for its yellow buds tea, the princess often led other nuns to pick and produce tea in her spare time. Later she learned that there is a kind of best tea in Baoerfeng peak, so every spring before the Grain Rain she led the nuns to pick tea there, carefully processed into tea. In 641 AD, before Princess Wencheng, the daughter of emperor Tang Taizong, married Songtsen Gampo (617-650), the 33rd btsan-po (leader) of Tibetan Empire, Princess Yuzhen gave her hand-made yellow-bud tea from Baoerfeng peak to her brother for taste, and a packet of tea seeds as a gift for her niece’s marriage. After drinking, the emperor gave the name of “Baoer Zhong xiu” to the tea and wrote in calligraphy. At the same time the tea was listed as a tribute. Every year 300 catty tea was sent to the palace.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
