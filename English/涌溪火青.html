Yong Xi Huo Qing Tea

Traced back to Qing dynasty, Yong Xi Huo Qing had become the tribute. A poet called Wang Chaolin in Qing dynasty gave a high evaluation to the tea. He praised that the tea is the best in Xuanzhou and its taste and fragrance are so attractive that people forget to return after drinking it.

Yong Xi Huo Qing is produced in Jing County, Anhui province, where streams flow around and trees flourish. All this, together with the mild climate, abundant rainfall and fertile soil, creates a unique ecological environment for the growth of the tea tree.

The tea appearance takes the form of round pearls, tightly rolled, These rounded leaves appear in shiny dark green with slight white downs.

The dry tea tinkles in the cup. With the flush, the dry leaves unfurl as booming flowers. Then these flowers will sink to the bottom immediately. This process is quite unique among all the green teas, full of interests. It can be steeped 4 or 5 times, with the second and third time as the best. It produces a light green-yellow liquor with a mellow taste. The very aroma varies with the places of origin, appearing such fragrances as orchid, sweet flower and others. But flower fragrance accounts for the most. The leaves turn tender, apricot yellow and lustrous after brewing.

In ancient time, there was a scholar named Liu Jin, nicknamed Mr.Luo han. One spring, he found a “gold and silver tea” (tea tree with half yellow leaves and half white leaves) in Yongxi. After picking it, because he could not find appropriate equipment to process, he put it into the pot for cooking rice and stir-fry it. Then a kind of tea whose dark green leaves are tightly rolled as pearls with slight white downs was produced. Interestingly, no matter the water is cold and hot, once the tea is put into the cup, it immediately sinks to the bottom. Then this kind of tea was served as the tribute to the emperor and its name was widely spread.

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
