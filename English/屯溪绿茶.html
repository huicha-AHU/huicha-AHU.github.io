Tunxi Green Tea

Praised as “ green gold”, Tunxi Green Tea is one of top ten Chinese teas. It is produced in four neighboring counties around Tunxi. Because teas from other counties were packed and shipped for export from here, they are collectively called “Tunxi Green tea”. With the passing of time this full name has been condensed into Tunlü (lü for green). The standard picking is one shoot with one, two, or three young leaves. There are six varieties (names in English: Eyebrow, Precious Eyebrow, Tribute, Needle, First Rain, and Green Flake.) in this tea and each has three grades. These variants looks different in appearance, but the mellowness is always the same. Back to Ming dynasty, Tunxi Green Tea had earn its great reputation in the international market. It had been exported to the West in 1913, hailed as the best green tea.

The main production areas are Xiuning, Yi, Ningguo, Jixi countries at the foot of Yellow Mountain and Qimen county. The tea gardens always locate in deep mountain valleys with abundant water. The tea are distributed on the hillside from 300 to 2000 ft in elevation. The fertile yellow earth,plenty of rainfall, and warm temperature provide favorable growing environment for tea.

The shape of the tea is slender and even. The color is green with gray hues. The tea soup is bright green.

The tea could be steeped for many times and its taste is fresh and mellow. It is refreshing and leaves a strong aftertaste. The aroma seems like Chinese chestnuts and orchid flowers. The leaves remain green and evenly after brewing.

After the middle of Qing Dynasty, Huizhou and adjacent Zhejiang-jiangxi border area produced green teas produced by the technique of stir fixation. Most are concentrated in place of Tunxi to refine, export, thus this kind of tea was named as “Tun Green Tea”, i.e. TwanikayTea in English. At that time, every peak season for tea production witnessed the emergence of bounds of Han and Hao ( shops for marketing) in Tunxi. Therefore, a ballad “ even you haven’t been to Tunxi, you can smell the aroma of tea. Once step into the concern, even the god forget to return” spread. Tunxi won the “ city of tea” reputation.  

Hui Tea “Going Out”

As early as in Ming and Qing Dynasties, Huizhou Tea was famous with the power of Huizhou merchants. However, it is difficult now for Huizhou Tea to go overseas. One of the difficulties is the cultural obstacle. The West knows litter of Hui Tea. The international status of Hui Tea needs to be improved. Under the background of “ going out” national strategy, the promotion of Hui Tea culture can lay a foundation for internationalization of Hui Tea. Hui tea has a long history and rich connotations. Introducing Hui Tea culture, as the first step of popularization, can help Anhui tea enterprises to “ go out” and bring new opportunities for the economic development of Anhui.
